Berlin is a city that has rebuilt itself several times within living memory, and it keeps the evidence. The street furniture layers the decades without editing.

GDR-era bus shelters stand next to 2010s cycling infrastructure next to Wilhelmine-era lamp standards that somehow survived everything. Nobody rationalised it. The result is illegible as a design system and entirely readable as a history.

I spent an afternoon in Prenzlauer Berg photographing things at shin height — utility boxes, kerb edges, drain covers — which is the level at which most of the layering is visible. The tourist level (eye height, monuments) is the least interesting part of any city's record.
