Writing publicly is a discipline, not a platform strategy.

When I write for myself, I can leave the argument fuzzy at the critical joints. I know what I mean. The reader — which is to say, the future reader, the one who will tell me what I actually wrote — is not there to notice.

The moment I write for someone else, the joints have to hold. "This is roughly what I think" becomes an embarrassment. The argument either stands or it does not, and the process of finding out is frequently uncomfortable and always useful.

I do not expect agreement. I expect to be told, specifically, where the reasoning fails. That is the most valuable thing another person can do for a piece of thinking, and it requires the piece to exist in a form they can engage with.

Comfort and clarity are not the same thing. I am trying for the second.
