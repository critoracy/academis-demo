The phrase "rule by Nobody" does a lot of work here that is easy to miss. Arendt is not describing incompetence or absence of authority — she is describing a structure in which responsibility is so diffused that accountability becomes impossible to locate. The bureaucracy functions; it simply has no face that can be held responsible for what it does.

This is the condition she thinks produces the particular kind of moral failure she documented in the Eichmann trial: not monsters, but administrators.
