There is a style of academic writing that hides its scaffolding — citations appear, conclusions are stated, but the reasoning that connects them is offstage. The reader is asked to trust a process they cannot inspect.

I have been trying to write the opposite way. If I am relying on a source for a specific claim, I want the reliance to be visible: not just a footnote number but a sentence that shows how the source earns its place in the argument. This slows the prose down. I think it makes it more honest.

The test I apply: could a reader who disagreed with the source still follow why I find it persuasive? If not, the citation is decorative.
