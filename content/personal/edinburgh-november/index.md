The light at 3pm in November in Edinburgh is not the light of a city planning to stay open much longer. The whole place has a reasonable expectation of imminent darkness and has made its peace with this.

I was there for a conference — two useful hours across two days, as discussed elsewhere — and spent the margins walking. The Old Town is best at that hour, when the tourist numbers have thinned and the closes are more or less unobserved. Advocates Close at 2:45pm with nobody else in it.

The view from the Calton Hill in what turned out to be the last fifteen minutes of usable light.
