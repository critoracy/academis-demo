Paywalls are not a content strategy. They are a funding strategy that has content consequences.

The criticism of paywalls — that they restrict access to information — is correct but incomplete. The more significant effect is what they do to the type of content that gets produced. When revenue depends on subscriptions, you produce content that generates subscription value: exclusivity, depth, a reader identity, a reason to pay. This is not the same set of incentives as producing content with the highest public value.

The publications doing the most interesting work on alternative models — grant funding, endowment, reader patronage at lower price points, institutional subscriptions with open access — are not getting enough attention.
