There is a version of every city that exists only between about 5:30 and 7:30 in the morning, before the daytime city assembles itself. The infrastructure is visible because there is no one using it yet. The street is its own thing, not a means to somewhere else.

I have been photographing this window in different cities for about three years. What I am looking for is evidence of overnight logistics — delivery crates stacked outside a shop, the cleaning crew emerging from a building, the single taxi idling at a rank that will be full in an hour. The invisible labour that the daytime city depends on and does not acknowledge.

Brussels, Ghent, Hamburg, Ljubljana. All photographed between 5:45 and 7:00, all in the period between autumn and early spring when the light does something useful at that hour.

A selection from Ljubljana in February is here. There was frost, a market being set up, and a man reading a newspaper in a café that had been open since five. He had been there long enough to have finished one coffee and ordered another. The city was being built around him.
