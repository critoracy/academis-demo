This is the founding statement of source-critical history, and it has been misread almost continuously since 1824. The phrase usually cited is *wie es eigentlich gewesen* — "as it actually was" — but Ranke's point was not naive objectivity. It was a rejection of teleological history: the idea that the past exists to teach lessons. His argument was that letting the past serve present purposes distorts the past. You cannot understand what happened if you are looking for what confirms what you already believe.

The irony is that Ranke himself was not free of political commitments. But the methodological principle stands independently of its author.
