Koselleck's distinction between *Erfahrungsraum* (space of experience) and *Erwartungshorizont* (horizon of expectation) remains the most useful framework I know for thinking about how historical actors understood their own time — not as a fixed point but as a ratio between what has accumulated and what is anticipated.

The piece linked here traces how this framework entered anglophone historiography and where it has been applied productively and where it has been stretched past its original meaning.
