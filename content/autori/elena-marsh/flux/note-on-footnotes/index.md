Footnotes in academic history serve two almost incompatible functions: they are simultaneously an audit trail (here is the evidence for this claim) and a parallel argument (here is the context the main text didn't have space for). When a footnote is doing the second job, it often means the main argument has a load-bearing gap. The solution is usually to move the footnote into the text, not to make it longer.
