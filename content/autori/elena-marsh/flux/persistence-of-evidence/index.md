A working paper on the epistemology of archival evidence — specifically on what it means to say that a document "proves" something, and how that claim changes when the document was produced under coercive conditions (interrogation transcripts, forced confessions, administrative records produced by occupying powers).

The core argument: archival evidence is not neutral raw material. It was produced by institutions with purposes, and the gap between those purposes and the purposes of the historian using the document is where most of the interpretive work happens. Ignoring that gap does not make it disappear; it just makes it invisible.

Currently at the outline stage. Target: conference submission by September.
