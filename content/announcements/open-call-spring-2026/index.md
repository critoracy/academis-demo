## What we are looking for

We publish long-form essays, research articles, and project documentation. All work must carry clear sourcing and a defensible argument. We do not publish opinion masquerading as analysis, and we do not publish analysis that ignores inconvenient evidence.

Our current editorial priorities are:

- **Digital governance** — algorithmic accountability, public data infrastructure, platform regulation
- **Urban history and policy** — how cities are planned, who benefits, who decides
- **Media and epistemology** — how information reaches people, with what effects on reasoning
- **Historical methodology** — new approaches to old questions, especially where archival sources are under-used

We are particularly interested in writers who work across disciplinary boundaries.

## What we offer

- Careful, structural editing (not just copyediting)
- Full editorial transparency — you will know who is reading your work and why decisions are made
- CC BY 4.0 licensing, which means your work remains yours
- No payment at this time — we are a small independent publication and we are honest about that

## How to submit

Send a short pitch (three to five sentences on the argument, not the topic) and two examples of your previous work to **submissions@example.com** with the subject line *Contributor Pitch — Summer 2026*.

We respond to all pitches, usually within three weeks.
