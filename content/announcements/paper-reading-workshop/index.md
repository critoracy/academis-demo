## Who this is for

This session is aimed at journalists, policy analysts, independent researchers, and informed general readers who regularly engage with academic literature but did not receive formal training in how to evaluate it.

We cover what you can and cannot learn from an abstract, what to look for in a methods section, when to be sceptical of a result, and how to find whether a finding has been replicated or contested.

## What we will cover

{{< cols left="50" right="50" >}}
{{< col >}}
**Reading efficiently:**
- The anatomy of an academic paper
- Where the important information is (it is not where you think)
- How to read backwards
- What to do when you hit jargon
{{< /col >}}
{{< col >}}
**Reading critically:**
- Sample size, effect size, and why the distinction matters
- What peer review does and does not guarantee
- How to find the criticism of a paper
- When to update your view and when to wait
{{< /col >}}
