## What we were looking for

For the inaugural issue, we invited submissions on any topic that met our editorial standard: a defensible claim, strong sourcing, and an honest engagement with the best counter-argument. We were interested in establishing the tone and register of the publication, not in defining a thematic focus.

## What we received

34 pitches from writers across Europe and North America. The quality range was wide, which is expected for a first call from an unknown publication.

We commissioned 8 pieces. Common reasons for declining pitches:

- The submission was a topic proposal rather than an argument pitch
- The proposed piece relied on a single source type (interview-only, data-only) without acknowledging the limitation
- The argument was not clearly distinguishable from the author's general perspective on the topic

None of these are disqualifying weaknesses in a writer. They are common early-stage habits, and we were candid about them in our feedback.

## Lessons for future calls

The pitches that were easiest to say yes to had one thing in common: they told us, in two or three sentences, what the piece would claim and what evidence it would rely on. They did not describe the territory. They described the argument.

We will state this requirement more explicitly in future calls.
